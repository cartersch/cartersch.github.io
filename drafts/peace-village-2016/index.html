What

Peace Village is a peace education day camp developed by First Congregational Church of Mount Vernon, Ohio in collaboration with Peace Village, Inc. The purpose is to teach and expose children (grades 1 to 6) to peaceful practices through fun, outdoor adventures, classes, music and interfaith stories of peace and fellowship.

When

Peace Village will be held August 1-4, from 9:00 AM to 3:30 PM. On the last afternoon (August 4th) families are invited to the closing celebration.

Where

Peace Village will be held at Ariel Foundation Park in Mount Vernon, Ohio.

Who Are the Campers?

Peace Village is available to 30 child participants all of whom must be going into Grades 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6. We expect to have Buddhist, Muslim, Jewish and Christian teachers. Children of all religious/spiritual traditions are encouraged to apply. Registrations are accepted on a first come basis starting May 15. Space is limited!

What children learn at Peace Village

Children will learn about:

  • Peace and the Planet: environment, ecology, nature
  • Peace and Culture: responsible thinking, media literacy, compassionate communicating, movies, TV, Internet, Bullying
  • Peace with Others: centering, calming the mind and body, meditation, breathing, concentration, movement
  • Peace Within: non-violent problem solving, role playing, peace advocacy

How much does Peace Village cost?

Peace Village is generously funded through resources provided by First Congregational United Church of Christ and private grants. Families are also asked to contribute a donation of $25 per child to help to cover some of the costs. If you are able to give more, we will gratefully receive it. If the cost is a hardship, scholarships are available.

What about lunch and snacks at Peace Village?

Children are expected to bring their own healthy lunch each day and will enjoy an extended lunch period for eating, talking, and playing together. Healthy morning snacks will be provided. (Note: If there is an economic need we can provide lunch for a child).
